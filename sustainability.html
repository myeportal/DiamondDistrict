Sustainability & Ethical Sourcing

Every diamond and gram of gold at Diamond District is chosen with respect for people, planet, and provenance. From Kimberley Process certification to RJC standards, Tracr blockchain, and partnerships in responsible jurisdictions like Botswana.

Our Commitment to Responsible Luxury

The diamond and gold industry has evolved dramatically. While challenges remain, systems like the Kimberley Process (KP), Responsible Jewellery Council (RJC), and advanced traceability tools have made verifiable ethical sourcing the standard for brands like Diamond District.

🛡️ Kimberley Process (KP)

Launched in 2003, the KP certification blocks conflict diamonds from rebel groups financing wars. It has drastically reduced such stones in the legitimate trade. We exceed this baseline with additional due diligence.

*Note: KP focuses on conflict; we also address broader human rights, labor, and environmental standards through RJC membership and supplier audits.

📍 Responsible Jewellery Council (RJC) & Traceability

RJC's Code of Practices demands independent audits for human rights, environmental responsibility, labor conditions, and ethical business across the entire supply chain. We partner only with RJC members.

Blockchain platforms like Tracr (backed by De Beers and now with GIA investment) provide tamper-proof digital passports tracking stones from mine to your finger. This level of provenance is non-negotiable for our collections.

Stories from Responsible Origins

In Botswana, diamonds are a cornerstone of national development — funding education, healthcare, and infrastructure while upholding high environmental and community standards. Similar commitments exist in Canada and Australia. We prioritize stones from jurisdictions with strong governance and verifiable benefits to local communities.
